Notes

  • Fragment 1 recto, line 2: Cf. Mt 26:75.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 3: On the phrase [π]ερι την πιστιν cf. 2Ti 3:8; 1Ti 1:19; 6:21; Ac 24:24.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 5: For εξηρανθη, see Mt 13:6; James 1:11.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 6: Cf. 1Pe 3:7.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 7: Cf. 2Ti 2:24.
  • Fragment 1 verso, line 1: This line is in a different hand from the rest of the verso and recto of the papyrus. For κουρ(), possibly κουρα, “fleece”(?)
  • Fragment 1 verso, line 2: Sarischouli’s options could be translated “was born” or “was made flesh”. Note παρθενου could also be translated “maiden” or “young woman,” but in Christian material of this era “virgin” is more likely intended.
  • Fragment 1 verso, line 3: Cf. Acts 2:24.
  • Fragment 1 verso, line 7: Cf. Mt 11:25; Wis. 10:1
  • Fragment 1 verso, line 12: Cf. Mt 28:2; Mk 16:3–4; Lk 24:2; John 20:1
