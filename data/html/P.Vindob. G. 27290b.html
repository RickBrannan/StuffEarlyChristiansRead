Suffering because of us, O Lord glory (be) to you.

He has risen from the dead, Savior, in three days … and all the unceasing forces of angels brought praise, saying, “You are blessed, O Lord, and praiseworthy and to be praised in song forever.” †

Notes

  • Fragment 1 verso, line 2: Note the papyrus has a break between verso lines 1 and 2, implying a new “song” starts with line 2.
  • Fragment 1 verso, line 6: cf. Dan 3:52 [LXX]
  • Fragment 1 verso, line 9: Note the papyrus has a line drawn from the last word on line 9 and across the entire of line 10, implying an end of the song.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 12: The papyrus has a line drawn after this line (line 12 verso) implying an end to one song.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 13: The Trishagion (thrice holy) is “Holy, Holy, Holy,” a reference to Isaiah 6:3 or to the hymn “Holy God, Holy Strong, Holy Immortal.” The reference here may be preceded by an invocation to sing, which the translation assumes. For an example of Trishagion, see P.Vindob. G 19887.
