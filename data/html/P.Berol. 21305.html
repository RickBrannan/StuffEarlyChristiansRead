Notes

  • Fragment 1 recto, line 3: For εκπ[..]ρ[..]ειν, possibly εκπορευειν? See John 5:29.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 4: For ενταυτα read ενταυθα. Note επτα (“seven”) occurs with frequency in 1 Apocr. Apoc. Jn: 2.1; 3.2; 18.2; 19.6; 24.5.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 5: Line ending could also possibly be τὸν πάσχον[τα].
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 6: For ἐτόλμησεν, cf. Mt 22:46 (also with ἀποκριθῆναί); Jude 9.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 7: For θειο. possibly θεοις (divine) or θειον (sulfur)?
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 8: Here κορυφῆς could be generic reference to person (Sarischouli 69).
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 9: Possibly a quotation from 1 Apocr. Apoc. Jn. 17.8, though the only similarity is a somewhat unusual phrase.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 11: ἐν τῇ ἀνατ[ολ]ῇ could also be translated “at its rising” (cf. Mt 2:2, 9)
