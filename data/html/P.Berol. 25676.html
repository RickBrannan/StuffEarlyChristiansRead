Notes

  • Fragment 1 recto, line 3: For π[άτ]ηρ ἐπίσκωπε read πάτερ ἐπίσκοπε.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 5: For [..]ννεος Sarischouli suggests the following possibilities: Διννις (also spelled Διννυς, Τιννυς, Ζιννυς), Δεννις, Τηνις, or Οννης.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 6: For αὐτὼν read αὐτὸν.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 7: For συναγώμενων read συναγόμενον.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 8: For ἰρήνῃ read εἰρήνῃ.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 9: For ποίησων read ποίησον
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 10: For πάτηρ ἐπισκωπος read πάτερ ἐπίσκοπε.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 11: For σαι read σε. Lines 11–12 can be idiomatically read as “I bid you farewell,” cf. Ign. Pol. 8.3; Mart. Pol. 22.1.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 12: For εὐχη̣μ(αι) read εὔχομ(αι). Lines 11–12 can be idiomatically read as “I bid you farewell,” cf. Ign. Pol. 8.3; Mart. Pol. 22.1.
  • Fragment 1 verso, line 1: This is the address of the letter, noting recipient.
