P.Fouad 203

Trismegistos: 63231

Transcription from: Pierre Benoit, “Fragment d'une prière contre les esprits impurs?” Revue Biblique 58 (1951): 549–565. English title is “A Prayer for Protection against Unclean Spirits.” The text is likely from a roll. Benoit has some fragmentary characters represented on either side of the column, but I have only provided transcription for the full column. This prayer is likely Jewish and not Christian.

And you are unclean, may he send you out to his messengers, who guided this people, who lead out, as he appeared to Joshua, son of Nun, and who cast you out into the abyss, into the place of destruction. Notes

  • Fragment 1 recto, line 3: The “us” (ημειν) should probably be “you” (υμειν).
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 7: Orthography is different than that of the LXX, but similarities with Josh. 5:9; 13–15 make “Joshua, son of Nun” the best option; though no options are good.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 8: The reconstruction ε[ι] does not make sense, but is the only option (van der Horst, 130–132).
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 9: The “us” (ημας) should probably be “you” (υμας).
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 12: “You will no longer be seen” means not being able to manifest themselves anymore (van der Horst, 132).
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 16: ἐξορκιζομένῳ could also mean “excorcising.”
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 19: Possibly συμπαρουσια.
