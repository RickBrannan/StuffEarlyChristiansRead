… with gladness you call the … glorifying him, the Master Christ, that appeared for the observers … For God will not ever cast away those who hope in him, just as even David the musician says, that “In affliction I called upon the Lord and he heard me, (bringing me) into broad spaces,” and that “The Lord is a helper to me and I will not fear. What can anyone do to me?” A(nd) that “It is good to hope in the Lord rather than to hope in rulers.” And again in another (place) he says, “The Lord shepherds me and nothing will be lacking for me,” and that “He leads me in paths of righteousness on account of his name.”

Notes

  • Fragment 1 recto, line 1: Probably either η]μων or υ]μωον. The ν in ε̣υφρο̣συν̣η is unclear in the published transcription.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 2: χριστ[ο]ν is not a nomina sacra. Unsure how to translate θεαριν, and the nu is very tentative as portions of the letter are illegible. Assuming a form of θεαρις (see LSJ, GE, sv. θεᾱρίς, “of or for observers”).
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 3: γαθος = καθως. Read: ου γαρ απωσεται ο θεος παντοτε τους εις αυτον ελπιζοντας. Cf. Ps 93:14(LXX); Rom 11:2.
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 4: μελλοντως = μελωδος. Cites Ps 117:5-6, 9(LXX). ωτι = οτι
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 5: Cites Ps 117:5-6, 9(LXX). ωτι = οτι
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 6: Cites Ps 117:5-6, 9(LXX).
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 7: Cites Ps 22:1, 3(LXX). ωτι = οτι
  • Fragment 1 recto, line 8: Cites Ps 22:1, 3(LXX). The ν in δ̣ικαιοσ̣υν̣ης is unclear in the published transcription.
